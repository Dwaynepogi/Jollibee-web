The History of Jollibee

From a small ice cream parlor to one of the world's most successful fast-food chains, Jollibee's journey is a story of innovation, determination, and bringing joy to millions of people.

How It All Started

Jollibee began in 1975 as a small ice cream parlor in Cubao, Quezon City, Philippines. It was founded by Tony Tan Caktiong together with his family. The business originally focused on serving ice cream, but customers soon started requesting hot meals such as hamburgers, sandwiches, and fried chicken.

After observing the growing demand for hot food, Tony Tan Caktiong decided to transform the business into a fast-food restaurant. This important decision laid the foundation for what would become the largest fast-food chain in the Philippines.

The Birth of Jollibee

In 1978, Jollibee officially became a fast-food brand. The cheerful bee mascot was introduced to represent hard work, optimism, happiness, and Filipino hospitality. These values remain at the heart of the company today.

Growth Across the Philippines

Throughout the 1980s, Jollibee rapidly expanded by opening restaurants in major cities across the Philippines. Its affordable prices, delicious food, and family-friendly atmosphere helped it become the country's favorite fast-food chain.

Going International

As more Filipinos moved abroad, Jollibee expanded internationally to serve overseas Filipino communities while also introducing Filipino flavors to customers from different cultures.
